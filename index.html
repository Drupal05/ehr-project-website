About EHRs

Electronic Health Records (EHRs) are digital versions of patients' medical histories, including diagnoses, treatments, medications, and lab results. They allow healthcare providers to access and share patient information in real time, improving the coordination of care and reducing errors. EHRs also facilitate data analysis for better decision-making and support public health efforts by enabling large-scale studies and trend monitoring. Their implementation helps streamline administrative processes and enhances overall healthcare quality.
